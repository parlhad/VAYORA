VAYORA ENVIRONMENTAL & HEALTH KNOWLEDGE BASE
============================================

== AQI BASICS ==
AQI stands for Air Quality Index. It measures how polluted the air is and the health risk level.

AQI 0-50: Good — Air quality is satisfactory. No health risk.
AQI 51-100: Moderate — Acceptable quality. Sensitive individuals may experience minor issues.
AQI 101-150: Unhealthy for Sensitive Groups — Children, elderly, and people with lung/heart disease may be affected.
AQI 151-200: Unhealthy — Everyone may experience health effects.
AQI 201-300: Very Unhealthy — Health alert. Serious effects possible.
AQI 301+: Hazardous — Emergency conditions. Entire population affected.

== KEY POLLUTANTS ==
PM2.5: Most dangerous. Enters lungs and bloodstream. Causes heart and lung disease.
PM10: Dust particles. Causes breathing issues.
NO2: Vehicle emissions. Causes airway inflammation.
SO2: Burning fuels. Causes irritation and asthma.
O3: Ground ozone. Causes chest tightness and coughing.
CO: Reduces oxygen in blood.

== HEALTH EFFECTS OF AIR POLLUTION ==
Short-term effects:
- Eye irritation, coughing, sneezing
- Shortness of breath
- Asthma attacks
- Headaches

Long-term effects:
- Chronic lung disease
- Heart disease
- Lung cancer
- Reduced life expectancy
- Brain effects (memory decline, dementia risk)

Air pollution causes ~7 million deaths globally each year.

== VULNERABLE GROUPS ==
Children: Developing lungs, higher breathing rate → higher risk.
Elderly: Weak immunity → high risk.
Pregnant women: Risk of low birth weight and complications.
Asthma/COPD: High sensitivity even at AQI 100.
Heart patients: Increased risk of heart attack due to PM2.5.

== HOME REMEDIES ==
Tulsi leaves: Improve respiratory health.
Ginger + honey tea: Anti-inflammatory, soothes throat.
Steam inhalation (eucalyptus): Clears airways.
Turmeric milk: Anti-inflammatory.
Amla: Rich in Vitamin C.
Jaggery (gur): Helps clear lungs.
Honey: Reduces throat irritation.

== INDOOR PROTECTION ==
Keep windows closed during high AQI.
Use HEPA air purifier (removes 99.97% particles).
Wet mopping instead of dry sweeping.
Avoid incense, candles, smoking indoors.
Use air-purifying plants (Spider plant, Aloe vera, Snake plant).

== OUTDOOR PRECAUTIONS ==
Wear N95/KN95 mask above AQI 150.
Avoid outdoor exercise above AQI 100.
Avoid peak traffic hours.
Wash face, eyes, and nose after coming indoors.
Change clothes after exposure.

== BREATHING EXERCISES ==
Pranayama: Improves lung capacity.
Anulom Vilom: Improves oxygen flow.
Diaphragmatic breathing: Deep lung breathing.

== CAUSES OF POLLUTION (INDIA FOCUS) ==
Vehicle emissions (major source in cities).
Industrial pollution (factories, power plants).
Construction dust (PM10).
Crop burning (Punjab, Haryana → Delhi impact).
Diwali fireworks (extreme AQI spikes).
Road dust (30% of PM10 in cities).
Biomass cooking (rural areas).

== WEATHER IMPACT ==
Low wind → pollution buildup.
Temperature inversion → traps pollutants.
Rain → reduces AQI.
Humidity → increases haze.
Sunlight → increases ozone.

== PERSONAL PROTECTION ==
N95 masks filter 95% PM2.5.
Stay hydrated (8–10 glasses water).
Eat antioxidant foods (Vitamin C, E, Omega-3).
Use nasal rinse to clear pollutants.
Steam inhalation helps lungs.

== SPECIAL HEALTH GUIDANCE ==
Asthma:
- Use inhaler before going out
- Avoid outdoor exposure above AQI 150

Heart patients:
- Avoid exertion above AQI 100

Children:
- Avoid outdoor sports above AQI 150

Pregnant women:
- Limit exposure above AQI 100

== FAST RELIEF ==
Eye irritation → wash with cold water
Sore throat → ginger tea
Runny nose → steam inhalation
Dry cough → honey + warm water
Breathing issue → move indoors immediately

== FUTURE & SOLUTIONS ==
Electric vehicles reduce pollution.
Green infrastructure reduces PM2.5 by 10–25%.
Government programs (NCAP) aim to reduce pollution.
Public awareness and behavior change are critical.

== IMPORTANT NOTE ==
Always prioritize:
- Reducing exposure
- Protecting lungs
- Monitoring AQI regularly
